// About

All my life I have always gravitated toward technology. In the beginning it was video games. Some of my fondest childhood memories involve my friends circling around a TV playing games on the Xbox or Playstation. Around my Junior year of High School I was given a "Python for Beginners" book by my grandfather and began to write very basic math problems or print my name.

It was around that time I began to form the idea that coding is something I really enjoyed. After attempting to go to college for biology I realized that programming is something that I should't keep as a hobby, but instead something I should pursue as a career.

Since then I have always been hungry to learn more. For a year I taught myself anything and everything in order to make development a career. When I moved from Michigan to Indianapolis I decided to take part in the ElevenFifty Academy JavaScript cohort, where the concepts and skills I began to learn could be sharpened and honed into something that could be viable in the workplace. I continue to be driven to learn more and master what I am already familiar with in order to provide the best product possible.
